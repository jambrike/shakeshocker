Overview of Othello

Othello, a tragedy by William Shakespeare, explores themes of jealousy, love, betrayal, and racism. Othello, a respected Moorish general in the Venetian army, secretly marries Desdemona. Iago, jealous of Cassio’s promotion, manipulates Othello into believing Desdemona is unfaithful. Othello’s insecurity leads to tragedy—he kills Desdemona, learns the truth, and dies by suicide.
